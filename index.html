Findings & Limitations

✓ YOLO excels at speed
~12 FPS on CPU is sufficient for in-vehicle deployment. Frames arrive at 30fps; we process every 2–3rd frame without missing a microsleep event.
✓ Minimal hardware needed
YOLOv8n (3.2M params, 5.2MB weights) runs on CPU-only edge devices — deployable in budget dashcams and Raspberry Pi.
⚠ Lighting sensitivity
Confidence drops under extreme glare or low-light. IR camera module or preprocessing normalization would mitigate in production.
⚠ Head pose variation
Extreme lateral head turns reduce bbox precision. A face landmark pre-filter (MediaPipe) would significantly improve robustness.

Roadmap

→ GPU / TensorRT export
RTX edge device or TensorRT would cut latency from 84ms to <10ms, enabling 100+ fps real-time with near-zero false negatives.
→ PERCLOS metric
Extend from N-frame counting to PERCLOS (% eye closure over rolling window) — a clinically validated fatigue severity measure.
→ Multi-modal fusion
Combine eye-state CV with steering jerk + heart rate (wearable) + yawn detection for a weighted ensemble drowsiness score.
→ Dataset expansion
Scale 1,081 → 10,000+ with synthetic augmentation (GAN night/rain/glare) to cover real-world edge cases and demographic diversity.
